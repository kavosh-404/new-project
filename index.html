Left Column

Model Overview

Kalick & Hamilton (1986)

  • Well-mixed population; no spatial constraints.
  • Random encounters among all individuals.
  • Matching emerges from preferences + simple rules.
  • Everyone has equal access to all potential partners.
  • Assumes fixed attractiveness-based preferences.

Smaldino & Schank (2012)

  • Agents live on a 2D spatial grid.
  • Local neighborhoods restrict encounter opportunities.
  • Mobility changes partners over time.
  • Density affects encounter rates and search difficulty.
  • Satisficing: agents relax after repeated failures.
  • Ability to switch between attractiveness- and similarity-based mate choice.

Right Column

Simulation Controls

What These Controls Mean

  • Preference Rule: Chooses how acceptance decisions are made. Attractiveness-based favors higher-attractiveness partners; similarity-based favors partners with similar attractiveness.
  • Mobility Level: Controls how far unmatched agents can move each step. Higher mobility increases exploration and encounter opportunities.
  • Density Level: Controls how many agents are placed on the grid. Dense settings increase encounter rates; sparse settings reduce them.
  • Selectivity: Controls baseline strictness in acceptance. High selectivity lowers acceptance probability; low selectivity raises it.
  • Patience: Controls how quickly acceptance relaxes as search continues. Fast patience increases willingness sooner; slow patience keeps standards tighter longer.
  • Exploration Bias: Scales movement range. Local keeps agents near neighborhoods; wide increases roaming across the grid.
